The Gap

Volatility is rising.

Work is faster. Feedback is louder. AI is rewriting identity. Small emotional misses compound into attrition, incidents, and reputational risk. Support exists — but utilization doesn't.

What This Is

A standard, not a program.

Programs are optional. Standards are operational. The Butterfly Protocol is a shared micro-behavior designed to be trained, measured, audited, and improved — like any safety system.

ISO-style · Scope

Scope

Workplace, education, sport, community — any organized environment under shared accountability.

ISO-style · Definition

Definition

A structured check-in triggered by observable behavioral signals, governed by versioned scripts and escalation rules.

ISO-style · Interfaces

Interfaces

Integrates with existing EAP infrastructure, crisis lines, and HR systems. Complements, not replaces.

How It Works

Signal → Script → Route → Measure.

Four steps. Trained in 90 minutes. Deployed in 30 days.

01Signal
02Script
03Route
04Measure
01 — Signal

The gesture that opens the door.

A trained observer notices withdrawal, tone shift, pattern break — or someone shows the butterfly gesture: "I need support."

02 — Script

No improvisation. No clinical language.

"I noticed [X]. Can I check in for 30 seconds?" Pre-decided language reduces friction to near zero.2

03 — Route

The right support, every time.

Peer → Manager → EAP → 988 → Local services. A decision tree so no one figures it out alone.4

04 — Measure

Data that earns organizational trust.

Adoption rate, routing frequency, follow-through. Aggregate only. ESG-ready reporting from day one.

Questions

Questions, answered.

A 30-second structured check-in for organizations. It trains a shared micro-behavior: detect signals, respond with a script, route to support, and measure adoption. It is a behavioral standard — not a clinical program.

It is a behavioral standard, not a clinical program. It operates upstream of therapy and crisis — at the level of early recognition and structured response. It routes to clinical support; it does not replace it.

The protocol draws on implementation intentions research (Gollwitzer & Sheeran, medium-to-large effect sizes), WHO Surgical Safety Checklist principles, and Deloitte's ROI analysis. Sources are footnoted throughout.

30 days for a pilot. 90 days for organization-wide deployment. Expansion decisions are data-driven — we recommend waiting until the first measurement cycle is stable before scaling.

Aggregate, anonymized metrics only: adoption rate, routing frequency, follow-through. No personal data. No individual tracking. The protocol is invisible to individuals and informative for organizations.

EAP is downstream — crisis response and counseling. The protocol is upstream — early detection and structured routing. They work together: the protocol increases the likelihood that employees reach existing EAP support.

Designed with GDPR, CCPA, and local privacy law in mind. A compliance framework is included in the deployment kit. Organizations are advised to review with General Counsel before deployment.
